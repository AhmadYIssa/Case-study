Redesigning the South Central Irish Wolfhound Club Website

South Central Irish Wolfhound Club, originally North Texas Irish Wolfhound Club, was founded in 1975 by a small group of Irish Wolfhound owners as a rescue and social organization. Membership is a diverse group and open to pet owners, breeders, those that show Wolfhounds or just enjoy social interaction with those that share a common interest and love of the breed. SCIWC became a non profit 501 (c) (3) in 2014.

Users

Folks in the 50-60s age range who love Irish Wolfhounds. Our community is based Around the Dallas, TX area.

Why Choose this Project?

Assisting with the South Central Irish Wolfhound Club (SCIWC) website design will significantly benefit the community by enhancing communication, fostering connections, and providing valuable resources.  A well-designed website can serve as a central hub for members to access information about upcoming events, health and training tips, breed standards, and other relevant topics.

Porto Persona

“The Retired Couple”

Age: 65 & 68 Retired Dallas, Texas

Joni & Jacob Adams

Retired couple, Joni and Jacob Adams, share their lives with Rocco, a beloved South Central Irish Wolfhound. Engaged members of SCIWC, they embrace a community dedicated to the breed, fostering friendships and learning. Their retirement is enriched by Rocco's companionship and the camaraderie found among fellow enthusiasts, making each day an opportunity to celebrate the joy and wonder of these magnificent dogs.

  • Goals: Learn more about their favorite dog breed, To make their dogs live healthy and happy little lives and Participate in dogs events.
  • Pain points: The lake of knowledge of Irish Wolf Hounds, The up-keep of a dog, it got a little harder due to age & their mobility and Not very tech-savvy, need the SCIWC website to be easy to navigate.

Empathy map

PAIN: Text too small, navigation confusing, Donation page is confusing - “It doesn’t say what the money goes to? The photo of the dog on this page is disturbing he looks like he is being held captive in a storage unit and that worries me but I’m not sure if the money is to get him out or keep him in” and Wishes there was more information on getting involved in events online.

GAIN: Likes the dog photos, Community events look exciting and fun to be involved with and Enough information to attend events

Problem Statement

SCIWC's website lacks a dedicated space for users to submit membership forms, hindering streamlined management. It also lacks clear calls to action, impacting member engagement and participation opportunities.
